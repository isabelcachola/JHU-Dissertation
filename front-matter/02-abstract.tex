\chap{Abstract} 


%%%% your abstract goes here (word limit: 350)
Automatic generation of summaries can help people make better decisions on what to spend their time reading, more quickly understand large collections of documents, or provide people with the most important information in news and science.  Broadly, the goal of automatic summarization is to generate a shorter text representation of a document or collection of documents, including the most crucial information in the input document(s). As shown with the COVID-19 pandemic, scientific summarization is particularly crucial to allow people of all backgrounds, from experts to the general population, to consume large amounts of scientific discovery efficiently and effectively. We hypothesize that, given the technical nature of scientific documents, systems that focus on the end user will result in summaries that better summarize and explain scientific documents.

Current methods often make assumptions about the intent and desires of the end user in order to constrain the requirements for building the system. However, these assumptions also constrain the potential utility the system poses to the user. With the advent of Large Language Models (LLMs) showing great promise in reasoning capabilities, we can leverage these models for a more personalized experience in automatic summarization. This personalization allows for summaries that both summarize and explain scientific information, improving the accessibility of scientific discovery.
